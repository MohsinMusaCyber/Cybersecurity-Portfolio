# Network Security Basics

## Overview

Network security is the practice of protecting networks, devices, and data from unauthorised access, attacks, and misuse. It combines technology, policies, and best practices to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.

---

## The CIA Triad

The CIA Triad is the foundation of cybersecurity.

### Confidentiality
Ensures information is only accessible to authorised users.

Examples:
- Encryption
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Access Control

### Integrity
Ensures data cannot be modified without authorisation.

Examples:
- Hashing
- Digital Signatures
- File Integrity Monitoring

### Availability
Ensures systems remain accessible when needed.

Examples:
- Backups
- Redundant Systems
- DDoS Protection

---

## Firewalls

A firewall monitors and filters network traffic.

Types include:

- Hardware Firewalls
- Software Firewalls
- Next Generation Firewalls (NGFW)

---

## IDS vs IPS

### IDS (Intrusion Detection System)

- Detects suspicious activity
- Generates alerts
- Does not block traffic

Example:
Snort

### IPS (Intrusion Prevention System)

- Detects attacks
- Automatically blocks malicious traffic

---

## VPN (Virtual Private Network)

A VPN encrypts traffic between a device and a network, protecting data from interception.

Benefits include:

- Secure remote access
- Data encryption
- Privacy protection

---

## Network Segmentation

Dividing a network into smaller sections reduces the spread of attacks and improves security.

Example:

- Staff Network
- Guest Wi-Fi
- Server Network

---

## Least Privilege

Users should only have the minimum permissions required to perform their job.

This reduces the impact of compromised accounts.

---

## Summary

Strong network security combines people, processes, and technology to protect systems from cyber threats.
